Introduction to the brothers

Erik XIV king of Sweden (1560-1568 Reign)

Eric XIV (born 13 december 1533 and died 26 februari 1577) was King of Sweden from 1560 until he was deposed in 1569 by his brothers John III and Charles IX. Eric XIV was the eldest son of King Gustav Vasa of Sweden and Catherine of Saxe-Lauenburg, while he was regarded as intelligent and artistically skilled as well politically ambitious. Early in his reign he showed signs of mental instability, a condition that eventually led to insanity. Eric having deposed and imprisoned, was most likely murdered. An examination of his remains in 1958 confirmed that he probaly died of arsenic poisoning.

John III King of Sweden (1569-1592 Reign)

John III (born 20 december 1537 and died 17 november 1592) was King of Sweden from 1569 untill his death at 1592. He was the second son of King Gustav Vasa of Sweden and Margaret Leijonhufvud. He was also the autonomously, the ruler of Finland as duke from 1556 to 1563 in 1581 he assumed also the title Grand Prince of Finland. He attained the Swedish throne after a rebellion agaisnt his half-brother Eric XIV, he is mainly remembered for his attempts to close the gap between the newly established lutheran church of Sweden and the Catholic church. As well as his conflict with and murder of his brother Eric XIV. His son Sigismund eventually ascended both the Polish-lithuanian and Swedish thrones as John III first wife Catherine Jagellonica of the polish-lithuanian ruling family.

Prince Magnus Duke of Ostergotland

Magnus Vasa (born 25 july 1542 and died 26 June 1595) prince of Sweden, Magnus became the duke of Ostrogothia in 1555. And many other holds like Kinda and Ydre, Dalsland, Sundbo härad in Närke, Kåkind, Valla and the major part of Vadsbo härad in Västergötland. As Duke of Ostrogothia he lived permanently at Vadstena Castle in Vadstena. Magnus was the only sons of Gustav Vasa who didnt become the king of Sweden if we discount his other brothers Karl and Sten who died in infancy. Magnus suffered from a mental illness, the illness showed it first signs in 1563 and eventually became permanent in 1574, which the responsibility of his fiefs were taken over by his brother John III who managed them as his guardian because of his mental condition. And on the side note John III granted a small part of his fiefsdowm was to his brother Charles IX.

King Charles IX of Sweden (1604-1611 Reign)

Charles IX (born 4 october and died 30 october 1611) was king till his death tough the story on how he became king and his conflict with his brothers and nephew will be relayed further down. Charles IX was youngest son and child of Gustav Vasa and Maria Elisabet. he was king of Sweden, head of the nation and de factor regent already in 1599-1604. When Charles IX passed away Sweden was in war on three fronts at the same time with Swedens neighbors Danmark-Norway, Poland and Russia.
